# Author's Note
I wrote these three stories while my own memory felt like it was betraying me. I spent four days arguing with myself about whether the third braid should be a noose or a life-jacket. Eventually, I realized it's both. The 'Forgotten Tides' started with a single image of a woman trying to carry a stone that weighed as much as a mountain but fit in her palm. It felt like grief.

## The Weight of Burning Things
Matter wasn't just catching fire; it was coming unglued. The stuff that held cups together and kept hearts beating was just... dissolving.

Cassia's footsteps were always heavy. She'd stepped on a piece of gravel in the hangar earlier and it was still rattling in her boot, a tiny, annoying click-click-click that refused to be forgotten.

It didn't displace air like lead, but it sat in the room like a secret no one could keep.

But in its essence.
